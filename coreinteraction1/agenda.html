INTERVIEW

pt.1 Find an example of an interview to analyze. Take screenshots (or scan/take photos of it) and document the URL. Try to find an interview that has thoughtful questions and is fairly in depth.

Create a document(Google doc, dropbox paper, PDF) that links to and shows the interview, and contains two sections called “Content” and “Design.”

 

In the “Content” section, write a few bullet points that evaluate how the interview is structured. How does it start? Does it start with more basic questions and then go deeper? What kind of topics and themes are in the interview? How do the questions lead the types of answers? Which answers are more interesting?

 

In the “Design” section, analyze the visual structure of the interview. How many fonts are used – write down the name of the font, the weight, the size, color, etc. Analyze the hierarchy of the interview. If any images are used, evaluate what they have in common, where they’re sourced from, and how they connect to the writing.

Submit your document to the google drive before class begins next week.

WEB DECONSTRUCTION

Choose a website that you love, hate, or find interesting and give a 5-10 minute presentation which articulates your opinion of the website.

Consider addressing:

• What is the first thing you noticed about this website?

• How is this website similar or different from conventional websites?

• Who is this website for?

• How do you navigate the website?

• How are links treated?

• What would your first step be in creating such a website?

• If you could change one thing about this website, what would it be?

STORY AS A NETWORK  

Pt.1 Jorge Luis Borges was an Argentinian writer famous for his short stories that deal with labyrinths, dreams, religion, and mathematical ideas (particularly set theory concepts like infinity and cardinality). His circuitous and meandering prose, full of allusions and vivid imagery, is a good way to think about the web as a network that has many nodes and many connections that continuously folds upon itself. It is the act of navigating through this maze that brings meaning to the web experience.

 

Read all three short stories from Borges' The Maker (1960): “Parable of the Palace”, “Everything and Nothing”, “His End and His Beginning”. Alt: read Glitch Feminism, a manifesto by Legacy Russell, Wendy Hui Kyong, Hito Steyrl, Katherine McKitterick.

 

For next week, read three texts. Write a short response on all three. Choose one text to work with on this project; think about why you are drawn to it and what creative affordances it holds. Include this logic in your written response.
